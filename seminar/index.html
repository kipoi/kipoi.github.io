Kipoi - Seminar

The monthly virtual seminar series is designed as a platform for interested Kipoi users and developers and will host talks on the applications of deep learning on biological data. The seminar is held on every first Wednesday of the month at 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m. CET. We are also happy to share the recordings of the seminar on YouTube.

How to take part

The Virtual Seminar Series takes place via Zoom. To take part in the seminar, you can register for the online Zoom conference here. Your personal join link will be valid for all upcoming lectures of the series.

How to apply as a speaker

The seminar is a great opportunity to present your recent work to a large international audience. If you want to apply as a speaker, please use the contact in the registration confirmation email.

Next seminar

Title: Deep learning approaches for the genetic analysis of high-dimensional phenotype data
4 May 2022 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m. Central European Time

Speaker: Francesco Paolo Casale, Helmholtz Pioneer Campus, Helmholtz Zentrum Munich

Abstract:

Cohorts with medical imaging and genetic data enable the study of human health and disease at an unprecedented resolution. However, the computational analysis of these datasets still presents several challenges, mainly due to the size and dimensionality of both genetic and imaging data. In this talk, I will discuss deep learning approaches that can enhance genetic studies of imaging traits. First, I will present an analysis of 4,641 H&E stained liver biopsy images from five Gilead NASH clinical trials, where we used a supervised deep learning approach to learn quantitative scores of NASH severity from liver biopsy images, which we employed in downstream genetic analysis of NASH progression to identify new candidate therapeutic targets. Next, I will discuss unsupervised approaches to derive imaging traits without labels—solely based on inter-individual phenotypic differences. Specifically, I will present the Gaussian Process Prior Variational Autoencoder model and applications to genetic analyses of neuroimaging data.
